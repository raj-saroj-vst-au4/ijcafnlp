Call for Papers

IJCNLP-AACL 2025 invites the submission of long and short papers featuring substantial, original, and unpublished research in all aspects of Computational Linguistics and Natural Language Processing.

Research Contributions Welcome

In addition to traditional research results, papers may contribute negative findings, survey an area, announce the creation of new resources, argue positions, report novel linguistic insights, and reproduce or fail to reproduce previous work.

Findings Publication

Papers not selected for the main conference will automatically be considered for publication in the Findings of the Association of Computational Linguistics.

Special Theme Track

Advanced Reasoning and Chain-of-Thought in Language Models

Explore key aspects of advancing reasoning and chain-of-thought capabilities in language models. We welcome innovative approaches to enhance step-by-step logical deduction, verifiable reasoning, and fact-checking methodologies.

Two-Stage Review Process

IJCNLP-AACL 2025 uses ACL Rolling Review (ARR) as the reviewing system

1

Submit to ARR

  • Submit articles to ARR for review
  • Receive reviews and meta-reviews
  • Double-blind review process
  • Option to make papers publicly visible
2

Commit to Conference

  • Commit reviewed articles to IJCNLP-AACL
  • Senior Area Chairs make decisions
  • Based on ARR reviews and meta-reviews
  • Option to revise and resubmit

Mandatory Reviewing Workload

Authors must specify which co-authors are committing to cover reviewing in the ARR submission cycle. This strengthens our commitment to the peer review process as research continues to accelerate.

Paper Submission Details

Long Papers

Length: 8 pages (+ 1 additional page for camera-ready)

Content: Substantial original research

Presentation: Oral or poster as determined by committee

Short Papers

Length: 4 pages (+ 1 additional page for camera-ready)

Content: Focused contributions or preliminary results

Presentation: Oral or poster as determined by committee

Submission Requirements

  • Two-way anonymized review process
  • Follow ARR submission guidelines
  • Include responsible NLP research checklist
  • Specify limitations section
  • Proper authorship attribution
  • Citation and comparison guidelines
  • Optional supplementary materials
  • Writing assistance disclosure
