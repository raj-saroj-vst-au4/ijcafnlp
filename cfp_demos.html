Call for System Demonstrations

The System Demonstration Program seeks proposals for demonstrations of innovative systems ranging from early-stage research prototypes to fully developed, production-ready solutions.

Open Source Priority

Particular emphasis is placed on systems that are publicly accessible as open-source or open-access solutions.

Industrial Innovation

We strongly encourage showcasing industrial systems that demonstrate technological innovation in NLP applications.

Best Demo Award

IJCNLP-AACL 2025's demo track features a Best Demo Award judged by a committee who actually interacts with the demonstrations during the conference.

Live Demonstration

At least one author is expected to deliver a live demonstration during a designated demo session, accompanied by a poster presentation.

Submission Requirements

Three components required for a complete submission

Research Paper

Up to 6 pages + 2 page appendix

Your paper must address these key questions:

What problem does the system address?
Why is the system important and impactful?
What is novel about the approach/technology?
Who is the target audience?
How does the system work?
How does it compare with existing systems?
How is the system licensed?
How was the system evaluated?

Submissions without evaluation may be desk rejected

Demonstration Video

Maximum 2.5 minutes screencast

Format Guidelines

  • • Screencast with audio narration (preferred)
  • • Video showcasing user interaction
  • • Production quality not required
  • • Used for evaluation, not published

Content Focus

  • • Demonstrate key features
  • • Show system workflow
  • • Highlight unique capabilities
  • • Include clear narration

Live Demo or Package

Strict requirement - no exceptions

Required Options

  • • Link to live demo website, OR
  • • Link to downloadable installation package

Exceptions

Only when special hardware is required and clearly impossible to share

Submissions without links will be desk rejected

Review Process & Ethics

Single-blind review with strong emphasis on ethical considerations

Review Policy

Single-blind reviewing process
Authors do not need to conceal identity
Include authors' names and affiliations
Self-references are allowed
No rebuttal stage this year

Ethics Policy

Must honor ACM Code of Ethics
Papers can be rejected on ethical grounds
Extra space allowed for broader impact statement
Sensitive data work must discuss ethical issues
Ethics committee reviews flagged papers
